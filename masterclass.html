SPRINGFEST MASTERCLASS

SpringFest Masterclass is a two-part event. Part one will be in-person on Friday April 30th and Saturday May 1st at St. Andrew's River Heights United Church, 255 Oak St. Winnipeg. Part two will be online at a later date.

Part One:

Advanced students in Groups A & B will perform their selections consecutively (with a collaborative pianist if the work requires accompaniment). The performance will be video recorded using high quality equipment and will be electronically sent to the professional clinicians.

After registrations have been received, students will be notified of the exact date and time of their performance. Students will be expected to arrive at least 15 minutes before their assigned time.

Part Two:

At a later date, groups of students will attend an online masterclass which will include individual and group feedback from the clinician.

Clinicians

The clinician for violin players will be Erika Raum. "Violinist Erika Raum had me hanging on to her every note" - The Strad. For more info, see andrewkwanartists.com

The clinician for cello players will be Blair Lofgren. He is described by Gramophone critic Ivan Moody as “a tremendous player". Cellist Blair Lofgren’s career is a marriage of extensive performing and dedication to his teaching. For more information about Blair, check out his website at www.blairlofgren.com

Audience

Depending on COVID-19 protocols, there may be opportunity for a small live audience. All COVID-19 health & safety protocols will be strictly adhered to.

Participants

Group A:
  • Students playing at a minimum of Grade 10 RCM level.
  • Students are expected to perform multiple works for up to 30 mins. Works may include a complete sonata or 2-3 separate works of contrasting styles.
Group B:
  • Students playing at a minimum of Grade 8 RCM level.
  • Students are expected to perform multiple works for up to 20 mins. Works may include 2 contrasting movements of a sonata or 2 separate works of contrasting styles.

Collaborative Pianists

Due to COVID-19 restrictions and health considerations at the venue, the Manitoba Strings Academy has arranged for 3 collaborative pianists to accompany all performing students. Students will have 2 rehearsals with the collaborative pianist (rehearsal times to be arranged by student) and 1 performance included in their fees.

The collaborative pianists will be Leanne Regehr-Lee, Hannah Smith, and Darryl Friesen.
